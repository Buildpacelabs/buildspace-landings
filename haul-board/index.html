Where the margin goes

Freight margin leaks between the load and the cover.

Every hour a load sits, the market moves against you. HaulBoard scores the four things that decide whether a move is covered on time — and at the spread you meant to keep.

Blind pricing High

Quoting off stale rate sheets instead of live spot data — money left on the table, or loads that never cover.

Carrier guesswork High

Coverage by phone tag and gut feel, not by lane history, equipment fit or reliability on the move.

Slow coverage Critical

Loads age on the board while spot rates climb — the longer to cover, the thinner the spread.

Margin leakage High

No line of sight on carrier pay vs. shipper rate per move — the spread slips, load by load.

Post → price → cover

From open load to booked carrier, in one pass.

HaulBoard closes the loop from a posted load to the carrier who should haul it — with the rate and the margin decided before you pick up the phone.

STEP 01

Post

Loads land from your TMS or a quick post. HaulBoard reads the lane, equipment, weight and dates and puts them on a live board.

STEP 02

Price

The spot-rate engine prices each lane on live market data — with a target margin and a floor, so nothing gets quoted below the line.

STEP 03

Match & cover

Carriers are ranked by fit and reliability. The desk books the best match in a click — and the margin is locked before the truck rolls.
