How a rent is decided

A rent isn't a guess. It's a weighted read of the market.

RentIQ decomposes every recommended price into the forces behind it — so the number always arrives with a reason your revenue team can defend.

Demand strength 34%

Live tour requests, leads-per-unit and search velocity by floor plan — the clearest signal of what a unit can command.

Comp position 28%

Where you sit against the nearby comp set — headroom below market, or a premium you're already earning.

Seasonality 22%

Peak versus off-peak leasing windows, modeled per market so you push in-season and protect velocity out of it.

Exposure & vacancy 16%

Units available and leases expiring soon — the brake that keeps an aggressive price from stalling lease-up.

Inside the engine

Sense the demand. Set the price. Guide the renewal.

RentIQ closes the loop from raw market signals to the exact rent to publish and the renewal offer to send — no spreadsheet gymnastics in between.

STEP 01

Sense demand

A demand model reads tours, leads, comp movement, seasonality and exposure for every floor plan — refreshed daily into a 0–100 demand score.

STEP 02

Set the price

A demand curve locates the revenue-optimal rent — the point that captures the most rent before lease-up velocity drops below your absorption target.

STEP 03

Guide the renewal

For every expiring lease, RentIQ proposes an offer balanced against move-out risk — with a projected acceptance rate before you send it.
